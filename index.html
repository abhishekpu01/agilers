MARKET SOPHISTICATION

What is Market Sophistication

Competitive markets are competitive for a reason. However, because there are so many competing products and so much advertising in those markets that it becomes really hard to earn any trust and attention. So, how can one stand out? This is when you need to understand the concept of market sophistication. First written about in Breakthrough Advertising by Eugene Schwartz, market sophistication is basically a term that addresses the level of awareness of your marketplace. In fact, there are 5 levels of market sophistication. At each level, the market needs to hear specific things to believe you. So by understanding your market’s sophistication level, you’ll be able to create a message that they’ll actually pay attention to and believe in.

Stages of Market Sophestication

No competition

It simply means that your product is new enough that it only needs to be placed out there so it can be known by your market.

Time to embrace yourself and increase it

At this point , you differentiate your products from the rest of your competitors by expanding your original claim.

The unique mechanism

At this stage, you’re telling the marketplace more than what you have and why you’re better than the others. You’re explaining how your product or service works.

Consumers are getting more skeptical by stage three.

Amplify your unique mechanism

At this stage, it gets more competitive. There are so many choices out there with people doing similar things as you. So now not only do you have to promise more benefits than your competitor, you need yet another mechanism.

Become Iconic

The marketplace place knows so much about the industry, they won’t buy into whatever you have to claim or hard sell anymore.This is where you sell on how your brand services only specific types of people and you encourage them to buy into the exclusivity.

There’s a shift from features to identifying who your customer is, who you serve, and who your product is for.
